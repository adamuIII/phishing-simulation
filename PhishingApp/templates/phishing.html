Phishing Attack

A phishing attack is a type of cyber attack where an attacker attempts to deceive individuals into providing sensitive information, such as usernames, passwords, credit card numbers, or other personal data, by posing as a trustworthy entity. Phishing attacks typically involve the following methods:

There are different types of phishing attacks, including:

  • E-mail phishing: Emails that may contain infected attachments or links to fake websites.
  • Spear phishing: A more targeted form of phishing, where the attacker customizes the phishing message for a specific individual or organization. Spear phishing emails often include personal details to make them more convincing.
  • Smishing: Phishing The attacker sends text messages that appear to be from a legitimate source, containing links to fake websites or prompts to respond with personal information.
  • Vishing: The attacker uses phone calls to impersonate a legitimate entity, such as a bank or government agency, and attempts to trick the victim into providing personal information.

How to avoid phishing attack:

  • Check URL: Before clicking on a link, check the URL. Often phishing attacks involve directing victims to fake websites.
  • Watch out for suspicious emails WMost phishing attacks begin with an e-mail. If you receive an e-mail that appears suspicious, do not open any attachments or click on any links.
  • Education and awareness: The most important step in defending against phishing is to educate yourself and understand what phishing is and what phishing attacks look like.
